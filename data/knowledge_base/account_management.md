# Account Management and KYC

## Account Types

### Savings Account

- Purpose: Personal savings with interest (2.5-4% p.a.)
- Minimum balance: Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 10,000 (varies by branch location and account type)
- Transaction limits: 3-5 free ATM transactions at other bank ATMs per month
- Features: Debit card, internet/mobile banking, cheque book
- Non-maintenance fee: Rs. 100-300 per quarter if minimum balance not maintained
- Zero-balance options: Jan Dhan, salary accounts, basic savings (BSBD)

### Current Account

- Purpose: Business transactions with unlimited transactions
- Minimum balance: Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 25,000
- No interest paid on deposits
- Features: Overdraft facility, higher transaction limits, cheque book
- Suitable for: Businesses, professionals, traders

### Basic Savings Bank Deposit (BSBD) Account

- Zero minimum balance
- Free services: Deposit/withdrawal, ATM card, monthly statement
- Limitations: Maximum 4 withdrawals per month
- Available to all individuals regardless of income
- Cannot hold another savings account with the same bank

### Salary Account

- Opened by employer for salary credit
- Zero minimum balance while employed
- Benefits: Pre-approved loans, insurance covers, higher limits
- Converts to regular savings account 3 months after last salary credit
- Minimum balance requirement applies after conversion

### NRE Account (Non-Resident External)

- For NRI foreign earnings in India
- Maintained in Indian Rupees
- Fully repatriable (can transfer back abroad)
- Interest is tax-free in India
- Joint account with another NRI or resident (on former or survivor basis)

### NRO Account (Non-Resident Ordinary)

- For NRI Indian income (rent, dividends, pension)
- Maintained in Indian Rupees
- Limited repatriation (up to $1 million per year after tax)
- Interest is taxable in India
- Joint account with resident Indian permitted

## KYC (Know Your Customer)

### Documents Accepted

**Identity Proof (any one):**

- Aadhaar Card
- PAN Card
- Passport
- Voter ID
- Driving License

**Address Proof (any one):**

- Aadhaar Card
- Passport
- Utility Bill (not older than 3 months)
- Bank Statement
- Rental Agreement

### KYC Categories and Re-verification

| Risk Category | Re-verification Period |
| ------------- | ---------------------- |
| High Risk     | Every 2 years          |
| Medium Risk   | Every 8 years          |
| Low Risk      | Every 10 years         |

### Video KYC (V-KYC)

- Available for opening accounts remotely
- Requirements: Smartphone with camera, stable internet
- Process: Live video call with bank official (5-10 minutes)
- Aadhaar-based OTP verification during call
- Account activated within 24 hours after verification

### Consequences of Non-KYC

- Account operations restricted (no debits, only credits allowed)
- Unable to open new accounts or avail services
- May lead to account freezing after notice period
- Re-verification required with branch visit

## Account Operations

### Opening a New Account

1. Choose account type based on your needs
2. Visit branch or apply online (video KYC)
3. Submit KYC documents and photographs
4. Provide initial deposit (if required)
5. Receive account number, debit card, and internet banking credentials
6. Processing time: Instant (online) to 1 day (branch)

### Address Change

- Submit new address proof at branch (or online for select banks)
- For Aadhaar-linked accounts, update Aadhaar address first
- Change reflects in 2-5 working days
- All correspondence will be sent to new address

### Name Change

- Visit branch with legal documents:
  - Marriage certificate (for name change due to marriage)
  - Gazette notification (for other name changes)
  - Updated ID proof with new name
- Process takes 5-7 working days

### Mobile Number Update

- Visit home branch with ID proof and written request
- Cannot be done over phone or customer care for security
- Reflects in 24-48 hours
- New number receives all OTPs and alerts

### Nomination

- Can be registered at account opening or later
- Form: DA-1 for deposit accounts
- Nominee can be any individual
- For minor nominees, specify guardian
- Nomination simplifies claim process and avoids legal complexities
- Multiple nominees allowed for some account types

### Account Freezing

- Reasons: KYC non-compliance, court orders, tax authority directive, customer request
- Frozen accounts allow credits but restrict debits
- To unfreeze: Address the root cause and submit required documents at branch
- Self-imposed freeze: Removable at branch with ID proof

### Dormant and Inoperative Accounts

- Dormant: No customer-initiated transactions for 24 months
- Inoperative: No transactions for 24+ months
- Reactivation: Visit branch with KYC, submit request, make a small transaction
- No charges for reactivation
- Unclaimed deposits (10+ years): Transferred to RBI's DEAF (Depositor Education and Awareness Fund)

## Account Closure

1. Visit home branch
2. Submit closure form signed by all account holders (for joint accounts)
3. Return unused cheque leaves, debit card, and passbook
4. Cancel all standing instructions and mandates
5. Transfer or withdraw remaining balance
6. Clear any pending dues or charges
7. Receive closure acknowledgment
8. Account closed within 1-2 working days

## Joint Account Operations

### Modes of Operation

| Mode                     | Who Can Operate             |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------- |
| Either or Survivor (E/S) | Any holder independently    |
| Anyone or Survivor       | Any holder independently    |
| Former or Survivor       | Only the first-named holder |
| Jointly                  | All holders must sign       |

### Key Rules

- Maximum 4 holders in a joint account
- All holders undergo KYC
- All holders share equal liability
- On death of one holder, account operates as per mode
- Nomination is separate from joint holding
- Tax liability based on the primary holder

## Government Schemes

### Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

- Zero-balance savings account
- RuPay debit card with Rs. 2 lakh accident insurance
- Overdraft up to Rs. 10,000 (after 6 months of satisfactory operation)
- Life insurance cover of Rs. 30,000 (for accounts opened before specified date)
- Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) for government subsidies

### Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

- For unorganized sector workers (18-40 years)
- Guaranteed monthly pension: Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 after age 60
- Government co-contribution: 50% of premium for 5 years (for eligible accounts)
- Spouse receives same pension amount after subscriber's death
- Tax benefit under Section 80CCD

### Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

- For girl children below 10 years
- Interest: ~8.2% p.a. (among highest for government schemes)
- Deposit: Rs. 250 to Rs. 1.5 lakh per year
- Lock-in: 21 years from account opening
- Partial withdrawal: 50% after age 18 for education
- Tax benefit: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) under Section 80C
